Practical information for a seamless and respectful journey through the Cultural Triangle.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress Code

When visiting temples, shoulders and knees must be covered. Wearing white is considered respectful. Remove hats and shoes before entering shrine areas.

Photography

Never pose with your back to a Buddha statue for a photograph. Always ask permission before photographing monks or locals.

Public Behavior

Public displays of affection are frowned upon. Pointing with your index finger is considered rude; use your whole hand instead.

Greeting

The traditional greeting is bringing your palms together in front of your chest and saying "Ayubowan" (May you live long).

Best Time to Visit

Season Months Conditions
Peak Season Dec - Mar Dry, sunny, and pleasant. Most crowded and expensive.

Low Season May - Aug South-West Monsoon. The Cultural Triangle remains relatively dry.
